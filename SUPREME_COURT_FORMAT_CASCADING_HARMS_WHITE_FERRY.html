II. THE CASCADING HARMS: LEGAL CAUSATION ESTABLISHED

Supreme Court Authority on Cascading Causation

Primary Authority: Hughes v Lord Advocate [1963] AC 837 (HL)

"You must take your victim as you find them" - Full liability for all consequences flowing from the tort

House of Lords/Supreme Court Precedents:

  • Corr v IBC Vehicles Ltd [2008] UKHL 13 - Depression from accident leading to suicide: defendant liable for full consequences
  • Page v Smith [1996] UKHL 2 - Primary victim entitled to recover for all psychiatric consequences
  • Lagden v O'Connor [2003] UKHL 64 - Defendant must take victim's impecuniosity into account
  • Simmons v British Steel plc [2004] UKHL 20 - Work accident psychiatric injury extends to all life consequences

Causation Analysis: Thin Skull Rule Application

The established chain of causation from the primary tort through multiple cascading stages is legally sound under UK Supreme Court precedent. Each stage represents a foreseeable consequence of the previous harm, without any novus actus interveniens sufficient to break the chain of causation.

The defendant's liability extends to all consequences flowing from the original tort, including Mr. Gany's increased vulnerability and susceptibility to harm from PubLove White Ferry's documented neglect.

BINDING AUTHORITY

"The defendant cannot escape liability on the ground that the injuries were greater than expected or that a different kind of physical injury from that which was foreseeable occurred."

— Lord Hope in Page v Smith [1996] UKHL 2
