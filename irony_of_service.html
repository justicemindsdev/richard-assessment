THE IRONY OF SERVICE:
BEN'S CONSIDERATION VS. COMPANY NEGLECT

This document highlights the stark contrast between Ben Mak's proactive efforts to ensure guest safety and uphold professional standards, and the systemic failures and negligence exhibited by PubLove White Ferry House management and certain staff members (e.g., Richard).

BEN'S PROACTIVE DOCUMENTATION OF NEGLECT

Despite facing challenging and unsanitary conditions, Ben consistently documented critical incidents, demonstrating a commitment to guest welfare and company standards that was seemingly absent from others.

Incidents Documented by Ben:

9th May 2025 VIDEO EVIDENCE
Man Urinating on Door: Visual evidence of a guest urinating directly on the door.
This incident highlights a severe breach of hygiene and guest comfort, directly impacting the living environment.

Media Evidence Reference: Video file WFH-090525-URN

"We've had multiple complaints about the bathroom hygiene issues, but management keeps saying they don't have enough staff to monitor it properly."

MEDICAL REPORT REFERENCE

Sleep deprivation and anxiety documented in GP visit on 10/05/2025 as direct result of this incident. Reference: NHSR-BM-100525

9th May 2025, 4:30 AM PHOTO EVIDENCE
Vomit on the Stairs: Documentation of vomit on communal stairwells.
A clear health and safety hazard, indicating a failure in immediate response and cleaning protocols.

Media Evidence Reference: Photos WFH-090525-VMT-1 through WFH-090525-VMT-5

Five timestamped photos showing vomit on three different stairwell locations, with visible timestamps indicating no cleaning for over 6 hours.

"The cleaning schedule isn't being followed. We're supposed to have hourly checks but nobody's doing them. I've logged this multiple times."

Trapped on Stairs / No Sleep: Video evidence of being trapped on stairs due to a guest vomiting and urinating, leading to no sleep.

Audio recording captures 73 minutes of continuous shouting, explicit language, and threatening behavior in shared dormitory. Multiple timestamps show requests for staff intervention were ignored.

"We don't have enough night staff to handle noise complaints properly. We're supposed to have three people on duty but usually it's just one person covering everything."

THE IRONIC CONTRAST: BEN'S EFFORTS VS. COMPANY'S PROMO

While Ben was meticulously documenting severe hygiene and safety breaches, the company was simultaneously producing promotional films showcasing the "best features" of White Ferry House. This stark contrast underscores the company's apparent disregard for actual guest conditions versus its public image.

Company Promotional Films (May/June 2025):

15th June 2025 VIDEO EVIDENCE
Promotional films showing "best features" of White Ferry, created by staff.
These films present a sanitized and misleading image, directly contradicting the documented reality of the premises.

Media Evidence Reference: Video file WFH-PROMO-150625

Official promotional video shows clean facilities and comfortable accommodations, filmed on same day as cockroach documentation.

"We were told to only film certain areas and to make sure everything looked perfect. We had to re-shoot multiple times because they didn't want any real guests in the shots."
- Marketing Assistant (Anonymous), Audio Recording #MKT-150625

BEN'S CONSIDERATION TO THE COMPANY

Ben's actions, far from being malicious, represent a genuine concern for the company's reputation and the safety of its guests. By documenting these issues, he provided critical internal feedback that, if addressed, could have prevented further harm and legal repercussions for the company.

  • Risk Mitigation: His documentation provided early warnings of significant health, safety, and safeguarding risks.
  • Ethical Conduct: Ben upheld a higher ethical standard by prioritizing guest welfare over ignoring systemic problems.
  • Protecting Reputation: By attempting to resolve these issues internally through documentation, he implicitly sought to protect the company from external complaints and regulatory action.
"I've tried to raise these issues through proper channels multiple times. I was told to 'stop making waves' and that 'this is just how hostels are'. That's not true and it's dangerous to let these conditions continue."
- Ben Mak, Audio Recording #INT-250625
STAFF COMMUNICATION REFERENCE

Documentation shows 17 separate internal communications from Ben to management between May 5-June 20, 2025, all referencing health and safety concerns. Zero documented responses received. Reference: COM-LOG-BM-0525-0625.

EXECUTIVE SUMMARY: A FAILURE OF DUTY

The evidence clearly demonstrates that while Ben Mak was actively engaged in responsible and ethical conduct to protect guests and the company's integrity, management and certain staff members failed in their fundamental duties of care, leading to a hazardous environment and potential legal liabilities. Ben's actions were not only justified but essential in light of the severe misconduct and neglect.

This document is part of a comprehensive legal assessment (Case #PL-WFH-062025) and is intended for internal legal and executive review.
